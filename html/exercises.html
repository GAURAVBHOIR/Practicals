Welcome to Data Analysis !!!

Now that you are well versed in the general aspects of X-ray astronomy, it's time for some real action. The previous sections would have given you a fair idea about the public data archive, related available software, and the fundamentals of timing and spectral analysis. Before starting off with the exercises lets get a clear idea of what we are going to do.

The data. All the data from diffrent observatories have been obtained through the HEASARC online service provided by NASA/GSFC. The lightcurves and the sepctra have been extracted from the downloaded data using the mission specific ftools . You are provided with these lightcurves and spectra and the exercise is to use the general (mission independent) softwares to analyse the data to glean important information about the various X-ray pulsars. We have used data from ASCA , BeppoSAX , RXTE and IXAE. We will advice you to observe the energy range and data quality of the various instruments/detectors (especially during the spectral analysis).

The software. We will be mainly using the xronos package (for timing analysis) and xspec (for spectral analysis), both are a part of the  composite software package xanadu . We will give you step by step guidance, akin a complete walk through of the programs, with the user input/response given in bold letters.

The X-ray sources. All the X-ray sources analysed here are, obviously, X-ray pulsars, and we will advise you to read the brief introduction about the individual sources while doing each exercise.
